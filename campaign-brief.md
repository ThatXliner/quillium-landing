# "Can't Think Straight?" — Campaign Brief

## The Tagline

**Can't think straight? Quillium works with that.**

### Why It Works

This tagline does three things at once:

1. **It's disarming.** It acknowledges what every writer already feels — that the creative process is messy, nonlinear, and chaotic. Instead of pretending writing is neat, it meets people where they are.

2. **It's provocative enough to stop a scroll.** The phrase "can't think straight" has a raw, slightly confrontational energy. It reads like something a friend would say to you, not a brand. That's the ragebait angle — people will want to engage with it, whether they agree or push back.

3. **It positions the product perfectly.** Quillium literally is the app for nonlinear thinking. Branching prose, forking sentences, keeping every version — the tagline isn't just clever, it's accurate.

### Provocative (Engagement Bait)

> **The app for people who can't think straight.**

Use on: Twitter/X posts designed to go viral. Lean into the ambiguity — people will quote-tweet to argue about it, which is the point.

### Long-Form (Landing Page / About)

> Most writing apps assume you know what you want to say before you say it. That your ideas arrive fully formed, in order, ready to be typed out. That's not how it works for most of us. Quillium was built for the way writers actually think — in loops, in branches, in "wait, what if I said it this way instead?" It doesn't straighten out your thoughts. It gives them room to grow.

## Visual Direction

### Color

Stay on brand — warm white (#f5f4f1) backgrounds, Quillium blue (#3b82f6) as the primary accent. Use the brand dots (blue, purple, green, gold) to suggest nonlinearity and branching.

### Typography

Newsreader (serif) for the tagline and headlines — it should feel literary, not tech-startup. Inter (sans-serif) for supporting copy. The contrast between the two reinforces the "serious writers, modern tool" positioning.

### Imagery Concept

Avoid stock photos of people typing. Instead, lean into the visual metaphor of branching:

- Forking paths
- Tree branches
- Diverging lines that feel organic, not sterile
- The Quillium editor itself (the branching UI is genuinely beautiful and self-explanatory)

### Tone

Confident, slightly irreverent, warm. Like a writer friend who's also really good at their job. Never corporate. Never "we leverage AI to synergize your workflow." The brand already nails this — the campaign should amplify it.

## Channel Strategy

### Twitter/X (Awareness)

Post the provocative version — "The app for people who can't think straight." — with the Quillium logo or a screenshot. Let the discourse do the work. Follow up with the product explanation after engagement peaks.

### Landing Page (Conversion)

Replace or A/B test the current "Prose for Pros" hero with the "Can't think straight?" variant. The subhead becomes: "A writing app that works the way your brain does. Branch any sentence, keep every version, never lose a draft."

### Product Hunt / Launch (Positioning)

Lead with: "Your thoughts don't go in a straight line. Why should your writing app?"

### Email (Retention)

Subject: "You don't think in a straight line. That's the point."
Body leans into the manifesto tone the brand already has.
